# Stock Market Volatility Analysis (Bachelor Thesis)

## Overview

This project investigates early indicators of stock market volatility, focusing on the S&P 500 index.

The goal is to understand whether alternative information sources can provide predictive signals for changes in market volatility.

---

## My Contribution

This project was conducted as my bachelor thesis.

I worked on:
- analyzing relationships between financial indicators and market volatility  
- applying statistical methods to identify predictive patterns  
- interpreting results in a financial context  

---

## Methods

The analysis uses statistical and time series techniques to study volatility patterns.

Methods include:
- Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression  
- Granger causality analysis  
- rolling window evaluation  

The focus is on identifying whether certain signals can act as early indicators of volatility changes.

---

## Results

The analysis shows that certain indicators exhibit predictive relationships with market volatility, although results vary depending on time periods and model assumptions.

This highlights the complexity of financial markets and the challenges of reliable prediction.

---

## Key Insight

Financial time series are highly noisy and context-dependent.

Even when statistical relationships exist, their predictive power may vary over time, emphasizing the importance of robust evaluation and cautious interpretation.
